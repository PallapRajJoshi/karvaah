Popular Destinations in Nepal

Kathmandu

Kathmandu Valley, a historical and cultural treasure, is one of Nepal's most significant tourism destinations. This valley encompasses Kathmandu, Patan, and Bhaktapur—three ancient cities known for their architectural marvels and vibrant cultural heritage. These cities, once independent kingdoms ruled by the Malla dynasty from the 12th to the 18th centuries, are rich with temples, palaces, sculptures, and arts that reflect the golden age of Nepali architecture. Seven prominent sites within these cities have earned UNESCO World Heritage status, showcasing their immense cultural value.

The valley’s legend adds to its mystique. It is said that Kathmandu Valley was once a massive lake surrounded by green mountains and inhabited by mythical serpents. According to the tale, Saint Manjushree, a revered Bodhisattva, cut through the mountain at Chobar with a powerful sword stroke, draining the lake and creating the fertile land we see today. This event made the valley suitable for settlement, and it eventually became the thriving center for various dynasties, including the Gopala, Kirati, and Licchavi (300-879 A.D.), under whom trade and crafts flourished.
