Overview

Farwest Scudurpashchim Province, also known as Far Western Nepal, is located in the farthest region of Nepal, bordering India to the west and north. This province is known for its diverse landscapes, from the lowland Terai plains to the rugged Himalayan foothills, offering a mix of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and adventure. Far West Nepal is relatively less developed compared to other regions, which gives it a unique and off-the-beaten-path charm for travelers seeking to explore rural and untouched areas. Karnali Province is located in the mid-western part of Nepal, named after the Karnali River, which flows through the region. It is known for its unspoiled natural beauty, rugged mountains, dense forests, and remote villages. Karnali is one of the least developed regions of Nepal, but it holds immense potential for ecotourism, trekking, and cultural experiences. The province is a peaceful retreat for those seeking adventure, tranquility, and a glimpse of traditional Nepali life.

Api Himal

Api Himal is located in the remote Api Nampa Conservation Area near the Tibet border. This stunning region is home to the towering Api Himal peak, offering trekkers breathtaking views of snow-capped mountains, glaciers, alpine forests, and diverse wildlife. Trekking in this area provides a chance to explore the culture of local communities, including Rai, Limbu, and Tibetan people.

Badimalika

Another key destination is Badimalika, a sacred religious site in the Bajura District. The Badimalika Temple attracts pilgrims and trekkers alike, offering not just spiritual significance but also trekking routes with panoramic views of the Himalayas, including Api Himal. The blend of religious heritage and natural beauty makes it a special spot for those interested in both culture and adventure.

Khaptad

Khaptad, located within the Khaptad National Park, is a tranquil escape, known for its lush meadows, rich biodiversity, and the sacred Khaptad Baba Ashram. The park is a haven for nature lovers, offering opportunities for wildlife spotting, trekking, and experiencing the peaceful spiritual atmosphere that has drawn visitors for generations. Dolpa

Dolpa is another must-visit region, offering some of the most remote and untouched landscapes in Nepal. Famous for the stunning Phoksundo Lake and the Shey Phoksundo National Park, Dolpa is a dream destination for trekkers seeking high-altitude treks. The trek to Upper Dolpo provides a rare opportunity to explore Tibetan Buddhist culture and pristine nature, with monasteries like Shey Gompa standing as symbols of the area's spiritual richness.

Bajhang

Bajhang, known for its rolling hills, forests, and traditional villages, offers a peaceful retreat for those looking to explore authentic rural life. The region is not yet heavily commercialized, making it ideal for those seeking a more intimate connection with nature and culture. Bajhang offers the chance to trek through serene landscapes, visit local villages, and experience the hospitality of the people.

Why visit Farwest Nepal?



Unspoiled Natural Beauty:
Both provinces offer some of Nepal's most pristine landscapes, ranging from lush jungles and rivers in Karnali to the high-altitude lakes and mountains in Sudurpashchim.

Cultural Diversity:
The provinces are home to numerous ethnic communities, providing rich cultural experiences and insights into traditional Nepali life.

Adventure:
For adventure lovers, trekking, rafting, and wildlife safaris are popular activities in both regions. Peace and Tranquility: These remote regions offer a peaceful retreat away from the hustle and bustle of urban life, making it an ideal destination for those seeking solitude and spiritual connection with nature.

Unique Wildlife:
With national parks like Shuklaphanta, Bardiya, and Rara National Park, both regions are rich in wildlife, including rare species like the Bengal tiger, one-horned rhinoceros, and snow leopards.
